Timeline

The start of System Adminstration

  • 1950s

    The history of system administration dates back to the 1950s when computers first began to be used in business and government. Early system administrators were responsible for the physical maintenance of the computers, as well as their software and networks. As computers became more complex, system administrators became responsible for the entire system, including security, performance, and user management.

  • 1970s and 1980s

    In the 1970s and 1980s, system administrators began to specialize in particular areas, such as network architecture, security, or software development. System administrators also began to take on more responsibility for system planning, design, and implementation.

  • 1990s

    In the 1990s, system administrators began to take on a more strategic role in the organization. They began to be involved in decision-making processes, such as selecting hardware and software, determining system architecture, and developing policies and procedures.

  • Present

    Today, system administrators are responsible for the management and maintenance of corporate IT systems. They are responsible for the security, performance, and availability of the system and its users. They are also responsible for system planning, design, and implementation, as well as user management and support.

Reflection & Learnings

Rhenz Nathaniel Fernandez

System Adminstrator

System Administration has been a process of learning for me. It is an ever-evolving field, and it requires constant updating of knowledge and skills to keep up with the latest trends and technologies. I have learned a lot from my experience in System Administration, and here are some of the most important reflections and learnings I have gained, Planning: System Administration requires careful planning and strategizing to ensure everything runs smoothly. It's important to take the time to evaluate the current system and carefully plan for future updates, upgrades, and other changes, Problem-solving: Being able to identify and solve problems quickly and efficiently is a key skill for System Administrators. It's important to be able to think logically and use troubleshooting techniques to find the root cause of any issue, Automation: Automation is essential in System Administration. Automation can help to streamline processes, reduce errors, and save time, which can be invaluable in a busy system, Communication: Being able to communicate effectively with all levels of the organization is essential to successful System Administration. It's important to be able to explain technical concepts and procedures to non-technical users and stakeholders, as well as being able to listen.
